What is it?

A nonprofit foundation building strong families through two frameworks: WELL and MADE. Filipino cultural roots. Goal: 1 million families.

Full name: WellMade Foundation

WELL stands for: Wisdom Empowering Love & Leadership

Tagline: "Building strong families through the WELL and MADE frameworks."

Vision: "Where every family has the tools to thrive across generations."

Part of The Living Love Collective alongside Dr. Jasmine's personal site and Pure Wellness Joy.

The WELL Framework

WELL is the philosophy. "A way of being." It calls each family member to lead with wisdom, nurture with love, and protect with intention. When a family operates in WELL, every decision, tradition, and conversation becomes part of something bigger than any one person.

How to start a WELL (3 steps)

  1. Gather Your Circle — bring together people who share your values and vision for family legacy. This is your WELL circle.
  2. Commit to the Framework — adopt WELL principles: wisdom, empowerment, love, leadership.
  3. Build Together — use the tools, retreats, and community of WellMade to grow.

The MADE Framework

MADE is the action arm. Structured programs where parents and children grow together. Every MADE program is inclusive, culturally grounded, community-driven.

MADE stands for: Mothers And Daughters Empowered / Moms And Dads Empowered

The 4 MADE programs

MADE to TravelFamily travel experiences — cultural awareness, independence, shared memories across generations
MADE to MoneyFinancial literacy for the whole family — wealth-building habits, economic empowerment
MADE in the BayBay Area workshops, circles, community events — local culture, family connection
MADE on MauiRetreats and healing on Maui — connecting families to land, culture, and each other. Transformative.

Cultural Roots — Filipino heritage

The Sampaguita Promise

The sampaguita is the national flower of the Philippines. It symbolizes purity, devotion, and the strength of a promise kept. It's the "living emblem" of the WellMade Foundation.

Under MADE, cultural roots run through two pillars:

Sampaguita (the feminine)Grace, nurturing, and the sacred bond of motherhood
Bayani (the masculine)Strength, protection, and the warrior spirit of legacy
